KEY TAKEAWAYS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

- **Stage exit criteria is the biggest systemic failure 
  across this pipeline.** Four of the eight flagged deals 
  have zero or near-zero documentation at stage 
  transitions — this is not a rep problem, it is a 
  process enforcement problem. Without mandatory exit 
  criteria, stages become labels rather than gates, 
  and forecast integrity breaks down at the source.

- **Three of four Commit deals are 🔴 RED.** Meridian, 
  Harmon, and Pinnacle are all categorized as Commit in 
  the source data but collectively carry 21 flags. 
  Commit forecast is currently overstated and cannot be 
  relied upon without immediate remediation.

- **Push counter abuse is concentrated in the RED deals.** 
  Harmon (4), Meridian (3), and Thornfield (3) account 
  for 10 combined pushes — a pattern that suggests 
  these deals have been managed for optics rather than 
  outcomes. A push counter policy with mandatory manager 
  review after 2 pushes would surface this earlier.

- **The two cleanest deals are also the earliest stage.** 
  Bluewave and Skyline are GREEN and the best-documented 
  deals in the pipeline — suggesting that process 
  compliance is strongest at entry and weakest as deals 
  age. A mid-cycle audit cadence would catch drift before 
  it compounds.

- **$175K Skyline Ventures is the highest-value 
  opportunity and the lowest-risk active deal.** 
  Prioritizing executive engagement and accelerating 
  the discovery process here has the highest potential 
  revenue impact of any action this week.
